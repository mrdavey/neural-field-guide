# World Models content-grade audit

Reviewed 14 July 2026.

## Gate and method

An independent grader read every rendered World Models lesson as a complete page: Orient, Learn, Try, Test, Extend, code, validation, and capstone surfaces where present. No final score was carried forward from an earlier pass.

The 100-point rubric is:

- technical accuracy and currentness (A): 25;
- beginner clarity and progressive explanation (C): 25;
- worked examples and analogy boundaries (E): 15;
- active interaction, diagnostic feedback, and retry (I): 15;
- depth, failure boundaries, and transfer (D): 10;
- deterministic assessment and scaffolding (S): 5;
- direct, honestly labeled reading and provenance (R): 5.

The acceptance gate is **at least 95/100 on every page**, not an average of the track.

## Material remediation

- Added an early World Models Orient block with outcomes, prerequisite activation, next use, and vocabulary before dense teaching.
- Replaced generic type-level interactions with 46 lesson-specific Change → Observe → Explain labs and diagnostic feedback.
- Added numerical free-bits, full backward λ-return, causal-standardization, Euler-step, matched-compute hierarchy, and separated horizon-versus-search-pressure traces.
- Made source kinds visible and stopped calling documentation or a secondary survey a primary paper.
- Added direct sources for Dyna, video diffusion, differentiable planning, planner overfitting, system identification and excitation, hierarchy, equivariance, multimodal grounding, and structural causal reasoning.
- Added source manifests to comparison artifacts and aligned the research-capstone JSON with its preregistered null decision.
- Corrected TypeScript math escaping and rendered lab labels/values through `MathText`.
- Made long inline/display mathematics locally scrollable and constrained story content at narrow widths.

## Accuracy and evidence boundary

The independent grader found no material technical falsehood in the final pages. Arithmetic checks covered the 0.68-nat free-bits charge, three backward λ-return traces, matched-call hierarchy ledger, Euler approximations, rotation residual, causal standardization, and the research-capstone null decision.

Browser simulations and numeric fixtures remain labeled as deterministic teaching evidence. They demonstrate mechanism and serialization; they do not claim external model quality, real-robot safety, or reproduction of published benchmark results.
